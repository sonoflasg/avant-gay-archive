**Evil Biscuit ([[Drifella]] series — especially [[Drifella 2]] & III) is basically the defining artist for the [[trait-maxxing]] aesthetic in the [[Avant Gay]] / Gay NFT scene.** [[Drifella 2 ]]is widely called one of the most influential collections outright, and the whole series takes PFP tooling ([[HashLips]] compiler) and pushes it into chaotic, reference-maxxed, painterly collage territory.

Unlike more restrained generative work, this is **pure traitmaxxing**: [[Drifella III]] alone has over 5,000 traits total, with individual pieces (or triptych "rooms") stacking up to 78+ layers. Think silhouette base (boy/dragon-ish Drifella character) absolutely stuffed with unrelated images, textures, memes, texts, pencil/paint elements, and internet detritus. It subverts the clean PFP format into congested, swollen, heterogeneous compositions that feel like digital painting or [[Dada]]/[[Surrealist]] collage.

### Traits to pick: Aesthetics & complexity > mechanical rarity
- **Go heavy on aesthetics and "looks maxxed"**. The scene (and the artist) resists gamification and homogeneity. Pick pieces that feel dense, spontaneous, destructive, and reference-heavy — the ones that reward staring and unpacking layers rather than ticking rarity boxes. "Chaos-layered, reference-maxxed" is the point.
- Specific vibes the community celebrates: Swollen with traits, disjunctive (clashing elements that somehow work), painterly misuse of PNG layers, dark/jester/absurd humor, nostalgia-warped internet sludge. Drifella III expands into triptych "rooms" for even more complexity — those are especially prized for demanding real viewing time.
- Rarity exists in the metadata (as with most HashLips gens), but it's not the driver here. People flex strong compositions and cultural fit over "1/1 ultra-rare trait." The best ones resist scrolling — they're not clean or minimal.

### What the Avant Gay community likes
This is ground zero for the scene's maximalist energy. Evil Biscuit's work defined the hyper-layered, tool-pushing style that many others (including crossovers with [[Parker Ito]] etc.) build on. Collectors love it for the anti-PFP rebellion: using the uncool compiler tool to make serious (yet playful, dark, ironic) art.

Look for:
- High trait count / visual density that still coheres into something compelling.
- Clever or chaotic layering (textures, memes, texts, clashing aesthetics).
- Pieces that feel like they belong in the broader lore (Drifella has narrative elements around desolation, death, jester energy, etc.).
- Ones that pop on custom viewers like [[Drifellascape]] for the full experience.

If you're going through the collection ([[Tensor]]), prioritize what hits you viscerally or makes you zoom in obsessively. In this crew, owning a fire Drifella signals you get the art-first, scene-first ethos — way more than floor-price chasing. It's the spiritual sibling to Tojiba's Horses: conceptual, community-core, and all about pushing generative limits in fun/weird ways.

Drifella 2 remains the consensus classic, but III takes it further into room-scale complexity. Pick what feels like *your* maxxed-out digital collage. The Avant Gay crowd will clock the good ones instantly. 🐉
