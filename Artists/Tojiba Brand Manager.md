**Tojiba Brand Manager** (Twitter/X: @m_m_____m____mm) is a central figure and one of the most respected artists/voices in the [[Avant Gay]] / Gay NFT scene on Solana. He operates as part of the [[Tojiba CPU Corp]] collective but is best known for his solo or lead work on projects like [[Horses? ]](Regular Looking Horses), [[Toji 100]], [[disc buddies]], and others.

### Artistic Style and Approach
Tojiba Brand Manager’s work stands out for its **conceptual restraint and compositional focus** amid a scene that often celebrates maximalist chaos ([[trait-maxxing]]). 
- **Horses** (his standout project) uses deliberately limited asset pools (ripped from a Russian horse builder site for bodies/attire, plus hand-built elements like hats, handlers, preheat, and textured backgrounds). This restriction forces interesting “sentence-like” compositions where layers interact meaningfully.
- Emphasis on **syntax over content** — traits function as structure/words in an image-sentence. He highlights subtle, humorous moments (e.g., Hat + Handler making the horse look like it just told a deadpan joke but keeps a straight face).
- Aesthetic: Calm, “regular looking,” soul-absent horses with painterly or tool-like presence. It explores animal PFP format seriously rather than parodying it, blending kitsch, melancholy, and irony.

He writes thoughtful notes on generation processes, the state of NFTs, and critiques scene pitfalls — positioning himself as both practitioner and critic. Projects often feel like hardware/software meditations or explorations of branding, loneliness, and digital surfaces.

### Influence in the Avant Gay Scene
- **Horses** is frequently praised as “brilliant” by peers (including Parker Ito) and sits alongside Drifella 2 as a cultural pillar.
- Tojiba CPU Corp as a whole (Toji 100, etc.) helped establish early Solana experimental energy with retro PC vibes and collective ethos.
- Embodies the scene’s **art-first, anti-rarity-chasing** values: composition, limitation as creativity, and conceptual depth over floor-price meta. His work provides balance to the more chaotic traitmaxxing side (e.g., [[Evil Biscuit]]).

Collectors and the community value his pieces for their clean-yet-layered feel, emotional resonance, and scene loyalty. He manually reveals horses in some drops, adding intimacy and sympathy for the process.

### Overall Vibe
Tojiba Brand Manager brings a thoughtful, slightly melancholic, and structurally elegant counterpoint to the scene’s punk absurdity. If [[Drifella 2]] is the explosive collage maximalist, his work (especially Horses) is the calm, tool-like horse standing in the pasture — observing the chaos with a straight face. Essential for anyone wanting the full spectrum of Avant Gay art.

Strong recommend going through his gallery or notes for the generation philosophy — it deepens appreciation for the whole movement.
