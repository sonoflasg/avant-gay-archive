**Bitcoin Puppets** is a prominent Bitcoin Ordinals NFT collection of 10,001 unique hand-drawn profile picture (PFP) puppets. It was created in MS Paint by the pseudonymous artist known as The Puppeteer / Le Puppeteer Fou / Monsieur Lasogette. Launched in January 2024 on the Bitcoin blockchain via inscriptions, it quickly became a cultural phenomenon in the Ordinals space and adjacent crypto-art scenes.

### Key Characteristics
- **Aesthetic**: Whimsical, absurd, lo-fi, and intentionally crude/hand-drawn. The puppets (often monkey-like or puppet figures) feature chaotic traits, memes, in-jokes, and surreal elements. It rejects polished or generative "fine art" aesthetics in favor of raw, internet-native absurdity and community lore.
- **Philosophy & Community**: Strong emphasis on "no roadmap, no expectations," organic community building, world peace memes, and the Viral Public License (VPL – similar to CC0, encouraging derivatives and remixes). Holders and the creator promote irreverence, anti-hype, and fun over financial promises. It has inspired extensive derivatives, merch, memes, and real-world meetups.
- **Token & Ecosystem**: Associated with $PUPS (originally a BRC-20 with a Solana bridge; migrating toward Runes). Often positioned as a mascot or cultural symbol for Bitcoin/Ordinals culture. Traded primarily on Magic Eden (Ordinals).

### Relevance to Solana’s Avant Gay Movement
Bitcoin Puppets represents a key mutation/fork in the broader "Avant Gay" / Gay NFT / Avant NFT ecosystem. It carries DNA from the creator’s earlier Ethereum project **Lasogette** (a 7,777-piece generative net-art PFP collection) and O.P.I.U.M. (an earlier Bitcoin Ordinals puppet project), then re-skinned for Bitcoin.

In discussions of the scene (e.g., Ryan Bethencourt threads and Parker Ito interviews), it exemplifies the shift from Ethereum’s polished EFAN-style fine art toward raw, chaotic, maximalist, subcultural energy—bridging Bitcoin Ordinals with the Solana-centric Avant Gay spirit of trait-maxxing, irony, overload, and rejection of traditional art-world norms. It embodies the "punk rebellion" vibe: camp, excessive, community-first, and deliberately illegible or unserious to outsiders.
