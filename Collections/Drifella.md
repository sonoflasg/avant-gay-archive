**Drifella** (full series: *Drifella*, *Drifella 2*, *Drifella III*, etc.) is a landmark generative NFT collection/series by the pseudonymous artist [[Evil Biscuit]]. It is widely regarded as one of the most influential projects in [[Solana]]’s **Avant Gay** (Gay NFT / Avant NFT) movement, frequently cited as *the* collection that crystallized the scene’s defining aesthetics. Worth noting Drifella originally dropped on [[Ethereum]] with Drifella 2 and III going on to drop on Solana.

### Key Characteristics
- **Aesthetic**: Extreme [[trait-maxxing]] and [[schizocollage]]—hyper-layered, chaotic collages built from vast trait libraries (often via [[Hashlips]] or similar compilers). Images overload the frame with memes, art references, textures, lore elements, and clashing styles, pushing the central “drifter fella” figure toward illegibility or abstraction. Later iterations incorporate painterly brushes, pencil traits, room-like compositions, and installation-shot framing.
- **Evolution**: 
  - Early versions draw from [[Mifella]] lore (a “drifter” evolution/fusion with [[Dratini]]/[[Pokémon]] motifs).
  - *Drifella 2* and *III* escalate density and conceptual depth (e.g., gallery-like rooms, self-referential scene nods).
- **Lore & Approach**: Rich, self-referential narrative tying into broader [[Avant Gay]] mythology. Biscuit treats layering as a conceptual tool—misusing PFP generators for [[Dada]]-like chaos and “destructive heterogeneity.”

### Relevance to Solana’s Avant Gay Movement
Drifella is often called the aesthetic cornerstone or “most influential collection in Gay NFT.” It exemplifies (and helped popularize) traitmaxxing/schizocollage as a rebellion against clean PFPs or polished art. Parker Ito has praised it as a pivotal inspiration that bridged net-art/post-internet practices with Solana’s scene, influencing his own works like [[Drilady]]. It embodies the movement’s raw, maximalist, experimental spirit—turning generative tools into vehicles for overload, irony, and new visual languages.

Derivatives, homages, and collaborations (e.g., with Parker Ito on [[Heavy Liquid Graphic]]) extend its impact across the ecosystem.
