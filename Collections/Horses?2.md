Horses?2 by [[Parker Ito]] (CreamyDreamy) is a lively, rapid-fire **homage/derivative** of [[Tojiba]] [[Tojiba Brand Manager]]’s original *Horses?* (Regular Looking Horses). Released on Solana (via [[vvv.so]], it’s a smaller, high-energy drop (around 777 editions) that quickly sold out and earned strong secondary demand in the Avant Gay scene.

### Concept and Style
Parker Ito created it as an affectionate, fast-paced riff on the original horse motif. It retains the calm, “regular looking,” soul-absent horse aesthetic but infuses Parker’s post-internet, painterly, and playfully layered sensibility:
- Horses with surreal traits, compositions, and references.
- Balances Tojiba’s restrained, sentence-like layering with more maximal or personally expressive elements (echoing Parker’s broader practice in [[Drilady]] and other drops).
- Feels like a loving evolution rather than a copy — “NFT war vibes” with insider referential energy that the community adores.

### Parker’s Own Words/Context
Parker has spoken about it directly:
- In his [[Verse]].works interview/journal (tied to The [[Pilgrim’s Living Room Crucifixion]]; Mona Lisa Hyper-Gamble), he lists *Horses?2* as one of his early Solana drops after diving into the scene. He frames it as part of his intuitive engagement with HashLips and the [[Avant Gay]] aesthetic — developing a personal workflow that matches how his brain works after being inspired by the scene’s trait-pushing energy.
- In the **Archetype Fund interview**, he highlights the original [[Horses?]] as one of the key projects that drew him in, visually grouping it with [[Drifella 2]] and [[Little Swag World]] This shows *Horses?2* as a deliberate response/homage.

As an established contemporary artist ([[post-internet]]/[[zombie formalism]] roots), Parker brings fine-art credibility while fully embracing the scene’s tools and irreverence. He’s described the Solana Avant wave as a revitalizing, [[Dada]]-esque moment for his practice.

### Scene Reception
It’s celebrated as a strong bridge piece: [[Tojiba]]’s conceptual elegance + Parker’s flair. Collectors value the referential nod, compositional strength, and cultural signaling. In lists of must-have Avant Gay holdings, it often sits alongside [[Drilady]], Drifella 2, and core Tojiba works.

If you’re browsing the collection, look for horses where the traits create memorable, humorous, or visually striking interactions — that’s where it shines.

**Horses?2** perfectly embodies the scene’s playful, self-referential spirit: derivative in the best way, turning admiration into new art. It’s a fun, high-clout entry for anyone deep in Avant Gay.
