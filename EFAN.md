**EFAN (Ethereum Fine Art Network)** is a loosely defined, informal scene (not a formal organization, DAO, marketplace, or project with an official website/Discord) referring to the polished, fine-art-oriented segment of the early NFT/crypto-art ecosystem on Ethereum.

It emerged in the late 2010s–early 2020s when Ethereum was widely regarded as the “fine art chain.” EFAN encompassed curated crypto-art drops, generative art platforms, and artists (often with roots in net.art/post-internet practices) who positioned their work within traditional fine-art contexts—seeking institutional legitimacy, gallery-adjacent aesthetics, and collector appeal rather than pure speculation or meme culture.

### Key Characteristics
- **Aesthetic**: Often described as refined and technologically sleek—“glowing lines and coding of tessellating shapes,” polished generative outputs, and work that echoes contemporary art-world sensibilities (as opposed to raw, chaotic, or internet-native styles).
- **Vibe & Goals**: Hyper-financialized yet fine-art-focused; many artists transitioned from earlier digital/internet art scenes and used NFTs to bridge blockchain with traditional art valuation and recognition. It felt “exclusive” and gallery-adjacent to critics.
- **No central hub**: The term is used retrospectively in discussions of NFT art history. No single marketplace, foundation, or token defines it (a separate unrelated ERC-20 token called EFAN/ETH FAN exists but has no connection).

### Relevance to Solana’s Avant Gay Movement
EFAN serves as the primary cultural foil for the **Avant Gay** (also called “Gay NFT” or “Avant NFT”) scene on Solana. The latter is frequently framed as a deliberate “punk rebellion” against Ethereum’s fine-art establishment: raw, chaotic, maximalist, camp, excessive, queer-in-spirit (regardless of sexuality), and allergic to polish or institutional approval.

Where EFAN echoed the contemporary art world and sought recognition within it, Avant Gay deliberately builds worlds “that don’t make sense to them at all”—embracing overload, collage, irony, trait-maxxing, and subcultural energy.

Artists and commentators (e.g., Parker Ito in interviews, Ryan Bethencourt in threads, and Solana creators like those around CreamyDreamy, notjohnlestudio, solanapoet) often cite burnout with Ethereum’s scene as a catalyst for migrating to Solana’s lower fees, faster pace, and freer experimentation.
