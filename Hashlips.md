**HashLips** (or HashLips Art Engine) is the **open-source generative art tool** that powers a huge portion of the [[Avant Gay]] / Gay NFT scene (and broader NFT generative art). It’s the go-to compiler for creating layered, trait-based collections on Solana and elsewhere.

### What It Is
- **Core Function**: You provide folders of layered PNGs (backgrounds, bodies, accessories, etc.), set rarity weights, and it randomly combines them into thousands of unique images + metadata (JSON).
- Originally built for straightforward PFP collections, but the Avant Gay crew **hijacked and pushed it to its limits** for [[trait-maxxing]] — hyper-layered, chaotic, dense collages that feel more like digital painting or [[Dada]]/[[Surrealist]] experiments than clean PFPs.

### Why It’s Central to the Scene
- **Democratized experimentation**: Easy to use, free/open-source → allowed artists like Evil Biscuit ([[Drifella]]), [[Tojiba Brand Manager]] ([[Horses?]]), [[Parker Ito]] ([[Drilady]], [[Horses?2]]), and others to quickly iterate and create wild outputs.
- **From clean to chaotic**: Normie use = modular, tidy PFPs. Scene use = stacking dozens (or 78+ in [[Drifella III]]) of traits, visible seams, references, and absurdity. This misuse turned it into a tool for [[schizocollage]] and maximalist expression.
- **Workflow staple**: Artists often run multiple generations, curate manually, tweak JSON, or combine with other tools for even more control.

### Cultural Role
In interviews (e.g., Parker Ito), creators credit HashLips with enabling the scene’s distinct voice — taking a “normie” tool and turning it into a vehicle for raw, referential, anti-polished art. It’s frequently mentioned alongside the rise of Solana’s low fees and fast iteration as what made the Avant Gay explosion possible.

Basically, without HashLips, you wouldn’t have the dense, meme-laden, lore-rich masterpieces that define collections like Drifella, Horses, or [[Heavy Liquid Graphic]].
