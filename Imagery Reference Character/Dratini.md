**Dratini** (in [[Avant Gay]] context) refers to the [[Pokémon]] creature (a serpentine, dragon-type "Mirage Pokémon" from the *Pokémon* franchise) repurposed as a central lore element and visual motif in the **Drifella** series by [[Evil Biscuit]] and the broader Solana Avant Gay ecosystem.

### Lore & Significance
- **Narrative Role**: According to scene lore, a [[Drifella]] is the soul/essence of [[Mifella]] (a degenerate Milady derivative) injected into or resurrected within a passed-on (dead) Dratini body. This fusion creates the "punk mash-up" character at the heart of the Drifella collections—adolescent/serpentine form with Mifella’s dark-jester energy. It symbolizes sacrifice, resurrection, mutation, and the birth of new scene energy (Mifella “dies” to enable Solana forks/expansions).
- **Visual Use**: Dratini motifs appear as base forms, layered traits, or symbolic elements in Drifella’s [[trait-maxxing]]traitmaxxed/[[Schizocollage]] compositions. It ties into broader [[Pokémon]]/IP appropriation common in the scene’s irreverent, memetic style.

### Connection to the Movement
Dratini exemplifies Avant Gay’s playful, lore-heavy, intertextual approach—blending mainstream pop culture (Pokémon), prior NFT mythology (Milady → Mifella), and chaotic aesthetics. It reinforces themes of transformation, illegibility, and community storytelling. The Drifella series (heavily featuring Dratini lore) is a cornerstone that helped define traitmaxxing and schizocollage, influencing artists like Parker Ito (e.g., Drilady homages).
