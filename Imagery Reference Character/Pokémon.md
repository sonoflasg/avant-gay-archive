**Pokémon in the Avant Gay / Gay NFT Scene**

Pokémon (the long-running [[Nintendo]]/Game Freak franchise) serves as **rich nostalgic source material and lore fuel** rather than a standalone collection. It gets heavily sampled, remixed, and mythologized in the scene’s signature [[schizocollage]] aesthetic — blending childhood IP nostalgia with memes, grotesque layers, NFT lore, and internet chaos.

### Key Connections
- [[Drifella]] Series ([[Evil Biscuit]]) — The most prominent example. The original Drifella narrative revolves around the death of [[Dratini]] (a Pokémon serpent) and its soul fusing/resurrecting with Mifella (from the [[Mifella]]/[[Milady]]-adjacent lore). This creates a darkly humorous, proliferating digital afterlife story. Drifella pieces often layer Pokémon fragments, sprites, or vibes into the dense, [[trait-maxxing]]traitmaxxed collages that define the scene.
- **Broader Aesthetic Fit**: Pokémon’s cute-yet-evolutionary, collectible monster design perfectly feeds the [[Avant Gay]] love of:
  - Nostalgic [[millennial]]/[[Gen X ]]detritus.
  - Grotesque/whimsical hybrids.
  - Chance-based or maximalist remixing (echoing Dada’s absurdity and readymades).
  - Self-referential myth-making (Pokémon as training data, evolutions, or fused beings in NFT narratives).

It represents the scene’s approach to pop culture: not reverent homage, but ironic, destructive, and proliferative sampling — turning corporate IP into raw material for outsider art. This mirrors how the movement treats other references (memes, vintage photos, prior NFT lore) to build “seams-showing” collages that feel alive and stitched-together.

### Why It Resonates
- **Nostalgia + Subversion**: Pokémon evokes innocent childhood collecting/training, which gets twisted into darker, adult, post-internet absurdity (death, fusion, digital multiples).
- **Traitmaxxing Synergy**: Easy to layer sprites, types, evolutions, or trainer elements into chaotic compositions without losing recognizability.
- **Cultural Anchor**: Reinforces the scene’s “sediment of online life” — turning mass-media childhood staples into tools for personal/cultural self-portraits in the 2020s.

In short, Pokémon isn’t a core “collection” like Drifella 2 or Horses, but a **recurring DNA strand** that helps define the movement’s playful, lore-soaked, reference-maxxed energy. It’s especially visible in Evil Biscuit’s work and related projects that fuse fan culture with generative chaos.
