**Milady Maker** (commonly referred to as **Milady** or Miladys) is a generative 10,000-piece PFP NFT collection launched in August 2021 on Ethereum by Remilia Corporation (an avant net-art collective/DAO). It features neo-chibi anime-inspired avatars with street-style, Y2K, and internet-culture aesthetics—cute yet edgy, ironic, and subcultural.

### Key Characteristics
- **Aesthetic**: Neo-chibi girls with varied traits blending kawaii, street fashion tribes, memes, and post-internet vibes. Often described as combining innocence with transgression or "cute aesthetic with cult behavior."
- **Community & Ethos**: Cult-like following emphasizing network spirituality, decentralized branding, experimental identity play, and counter-mainstream energy. It includes lore-building (e.g., Minecraft servers like Miladycraft), events (e.g., raves), derivatives, and a "copylefted brand" philosophy encouraging remixes. Associated with $LADYS meme coin (community-driven, not official) and spin-offs like Remilio Babies or Bonkler.
- **Creator**: Led by [[Charlotte Fang]] (pseudonymous/controversial figure tied to earlier online personas and scenes). Remilia positioned it as an experiment in on-chain culture beyond traditional NFTs.

### Relevance to Solana’s Avant Gay Movement
Milady is widely regarded as a foundational precursor and "shock to Ethereum’s ([[EFAN]]) system" for the broader [[Avant Gay]] / Gay NFT / Avant NFT scene. Its edgy, ironic, 4chan-adjacent internet sensibility, cult community dynamics, and rejection of polished mainstream NFT culture provided key DNA for the movement.

While originating on Ethereum, its rebellious ethos "jumped chains" and influenced Solana projects like **MiFella** (a degenerate/homage offshoot). Commentators describe the [[Avant Gay]] scene on Solana as a "punk rebellion" that mutated and amplified Milady-style energy into raw, chaotic, maximalist, queer-spirited, and outsider art—contrasting [[EFAN]]'s fine-art polish.

It helped seed the subcultural, anti-institutional, [[trait-maxxing]], and lore-heavy approach that defines much of Solana Avant Gay (alongside projects like those from [[Tojiba]], [[Evil Biscuit]], etc.).
